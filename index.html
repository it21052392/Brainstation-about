Literature Survey: ADHD

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) affects approximately 5-7% of children and 2-5% of adults worldwide, impacting academic, occupational, and social functioning. Once viewed primarily as a childhood disorder, ADHD is now recognized to persist into adulthood.

Advances in technology have enhanced ADHD diagnosis and management, shifting from subjective methods to objective tools like real-time emotion detection and head movement tracking. These improvements highlight the need for adaptive learning tools tailored to ADHD, which utilize machine learning to customize educational experiences and provide instant feedback.

Additionally, the role of emotion dysregulation in ADHD, linked to impulsivity, is crucial for developing effective interventions across all ages. Our research focuses on these areas to improve outcomes for individuals with ADHD.

The main research focuses are:

  • Real-time emotion detection and head movement tracking to improve ADHD diagnosis.
  • Development of adaptive learning tools tailored to ADHD using machine learning.
  • Research on the role of emotion dysregulation in ADHD and its connection to impulsivity.
  • Implementation of interventions for children and adults with ADHD.

Research Gap: ADHD in Educational Technologies

Despite significant advances in ADHD treatments, our understanding of how educational technologies can better serve students with ADHD remains limited. Current tools often overlook the dynamic nature of ADHD symptoms, particularly in academic settings, where real-time monitoring and personalized interventions could greatly enhance focus and engagement.

Notably, inattentive ADHD in digital learning environments is underexplored, especially at the university level. Many educational platforms fail to offer ADHD-specific strategies, leaving a critical need for adaptive learning systems that can dynamically adjust to students' cognitive states and provide immediate feedback.

Our research aims to bridge these gaps by developing sophisticated, real-time systems that cater to the unique learning needs of students with ADHD. We are incorporating advanced cognitive theories to ensure that learning materials are effectively tailored and supportive.

  • Developing adaptive learning tools that adjust dynamically to students' cognitive states.
  • Providing real-time interventions to enhance focus and engagement in digital learning environments.
  • Addressing inattentive ADHD, particularly at the university level, through tailored digital solutions.
  • Incorporating advanced cognitive theories into ADHD-specific educational platforms.

Research Problem: ADHD and Adaptive Learning Technologies

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) significantly impacts students in academic, occupational, and social settings, especially at the university level where challenges like sustained attention and impulse control are magnified.

This research explores the inadequacies of traditional ADHD diagnostic methods and educational frameworks, and the potential of adaptive learning technologies to better support these students.

Traditional tools often miss the dynamic nature of ADHD symptoms in real settings, failing to offer real-time monitoring or personalized interventions. Our project aims to integrate advanced technologies such as AI and real-time monitoring into learning platforms to create adaptive systems that respond dynamically to students' needs, enhancing engagement and academic performance.

  • Exploring the limitations of traditional ADHD diagnostic and management methods in real academic settings.
  • Developing AI-based systems for real-time monitoring of ADHD symptoms during learning sessions.
  • Creating adaptive learning tools that dynamically adjust to students’ cognitive and emotional states to improve learning outcomes.

Research Objectives: Developing an Adaptive Learning Tool for ADHD

Our research is dedicated to developing an adaptive learning tool specifically for students with ADHD, aiming to enhance engagement, academic performance, and memory retention through personalized interventions.

This tool will dynamically adjust learning activities based on real-time attention, focus, and emotional data. The key components of our approach include:

  • Adaptive Quizzing System: Uses spaced repetition to optimize quiz timing and difficulty, improving knowledge retention.
  • Personalized ADHD Identifier: Analyzes individual ADHD profiles to tailor learning experiences.
  • Break Recommendation System: Suggests timely breaks to manage focus and reduce burnout.
  • Gamified Task Completion Strategies: Implements gamification to motivate students and make learning more engaging.
  • ADHD-Friendly Educational Content: Includes interactive elements like mind maps, visual aids, and simplified text to support diverse learning styles.

This comprehensive approach ensures that learning is tailored to the cognitive needs of neurodivergent learners, bridging the gap between traditional education models and the unique requirements of ADHD students.

Research Methodology

Our research methodology develops an adaptive learning system tailored for students with ADHD, incorporating four key components:

  • Content Chunking and Simplification with Interactive Content: Uses NLP and machine learning to transform complex information into ADHD-friendly formats, visualized through interactive mind maps.
  • Adaptive Quizzing System: Employs spaced repetition and adaptive algorithms to enhance memory retention by personalizing quiz difficulty and timing based on student performance.
  • Personalized ADHD Identifier and Break Recommendation System: Analyzes real-time behavioral data to recommend tailored breaks and adjust learning sessions to help manage focus.
  • Maximizing Learning Potential through an Adaptive Algorithm: Dynamically adjusts educational content and tasks by analyzing academic, behavioral, and emotional data to maximize engagement and academic outcomes.

This comprehensive system is designed to address the diverse challenges faced by ADHD learners, enhancing their educational experience through advanced analytics and adaptive technologies.

Technologies Used

Our project employs a variety of advanced technologies across different areas:

  • Python: Primary programming language for the project.
  • OpenCV: For capturing video frames from webcams in real-time.
  • Dlib: Used for facial landmark detection.
  • Keras: For loading pre-trained deep learning models that predict emotional states.
  • FastAPI: For managing communication between front-end and back-end systems
  • MongoDB: Database used for storing processed data.
  • Flask: Backend framework for managing server-side logic.
  • React.js: Used for frontend development.
  • Tailwind CSS: For styling the frontend.
  • Redux: For state management across the frontend.
  • Node.js: For backend development in some components.
  • Jenkins: For automating testing and deployment.

Content Chunking and Simplification with Interactive Content

This feature employs natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning technologies to deconstruct and simplify dense educational materials into formats more accessible for students with ADHD. It integrates a custom-trained T5 model to adapt text into simpler structures and OpenAI’s GPT-4 to enrich the content, ensuring comprehensibility without sacrificing depth. The resulting data is visualized through interactive mind maps using D3.js and SVG, providing a dynamic and engaging way for students to navigate and absorb complex concepts effectively.

Adaptive Quizzing System

The adaptive quizzing feature leverages advanced machine learning algorithms, including a custom implementation of the spaced repetition technique, to dynamically adjust the timing and complexity of quizzes based on the student’s performance and cognitive state. This method ensures that quizzes are challenging yet manageable, promoting better long-term retention and cognitive engagement by adapting in real-time to the learner's needs, making education more responsive and effective for ADHD students.

Personalized ADHD Identifier and Break Recommendation System

This feature integrates real-time data analysis to monitor students' attention and emotional states continuously. By employing algorithms that detect signs of focus loss or emotional overwhelm, the system can intervene at critical moments to suggest personalized breaks, allowing students to recharge and regain concentration. This proactive approach helps manage the unique attentional challenges of ADHD learners, providing support precisely when needed to maintain optimal learning engagement

Maximizing Learning Potential through an Adaptive Algorithm

Focusing on maximizing learning potential, this feature uses a comprehensive adaptive algorithm that collects and processes academic, behavioral, and emotional data from student interactions. By continuously analyzing this data, the system customizes the learning experience to match individual profiles, adjusting tasks and content in real-time. This adaptive approach helps to enhance engagement, understanding, and academic performance by ensuring that educational interventions are aligned with each student’s current needs and capabilities, thereby supporting sustained academic success for students with ADHD
